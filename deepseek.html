About Me

Engineering Mindset,
Creative Spirit

I'm an undergraduate Civil Engineering student from Nepal, drawn to the intersection of transportation infrastructure, geotechnical engineering, and sustainable development. My journey began with a fascination for roads, bridges, and the invisible systems that connect communities across challenging terrain.

I believe great infrastructure is both technically sound and deeply human. Every road, every bridge, every retaining wall serves people — and that responsibility drives my commitment to rigorous learning and thoughtful design.

"I'm not here to pretend I know everything. I'm here to document what I learn, build what I can, and grow into the transportation engineer I aim to become."

Beyond engineering, I enjoy programming, graphic design, and organizing college events. These interests complement my technical studies — they teach me to communicate clearly, think systematically, and work collaboratively.

Research

Research Interests

Areas I'm actively exploring and hope to contribute to in the future.

🚦 Transportation Safety in Mountainous Terrain

Investigating how road geometry, geotechnical conditions, and weather interact to affect safety on Nepal's hill roads. Early-stage literature review in progress.

🌍 Sustainable Infrastructure Development

Exploring low-impact road construction techniques suitable for environmentally sensitive regions — balancing development with ecological preservation.

🪨 Slope Stability & Retaining Structures

Studying geotechnical approaches to slope stabilization in the Himalayan foothills, where landslides pose significant challenges to infrastructure longevity.

📡 GIS Applications in Highway Planning

Learning how Geographic Information Systems can optimize route selection and terrain analysis for future highway projects in Nepal.
